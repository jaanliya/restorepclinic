Hands-On Expertise

Advanced Manual Therapy Techniques

Our therapists are trained in the world's most effective hands-on physiotherapy techniques — delivering precise, evidence-based treatment for faster and lasting recovery.

01

Maitland Technique

A globally recognised passive joint mobilisation method using graded oscillatory movements to restore joint range, reduce pain, and improve function — applied with precision at specific vertebral and peripheral joints.

Joint Mobilisation Spinal Pain Graded Oscillation
02

Mulligan Technique

Combines sustained natural apophyseal glides (SNAGs) with active patient movement to achieve immediate pain-free motion. Highly effective for spinal and peripheral joint dysfunctions with rapid, lasting results.

SNAGs NAGs Pain-Free Movement
03

McKenzie Technique

A mechanical diagnosis and therapy (MDT) system using directional preference exercises to centralise and eliminate disc-related pain. Empowers patients with self-treatment strategies for long-term independence.

MDT Disc Pain Centralisation
04

Joint Mobilization & Manipulation

Skilled passive techniques ranging from gentle Grade I–IV mobilisations to high-velocity low-amplitude (HVLA) manipulations — restoring joint play, reducing stiffness, and relieving neurological symptoms.

HVLA Grade I–IV Joint Stiffness
05

Soft Tissue Mobilization

Targeted hands-on techniques including myofascial release, trigger point therapy, and cross-friction massage to break down scar tissue, reduce muscle tightness, and restore normal tissue extensibility.

Myofascial Release Trigger Points Scar Tissue
06

Muscle Energy Techniques

Post-isometric relaxation and reciprocal inhibition methods using the patient's own muscle contractions to lengthen tight muscles, restore joint range of motion, and correct musculoskeletal imbalances.

Post-Isometric Muscle Lengthening ROM Restoration
